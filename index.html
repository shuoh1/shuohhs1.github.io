About

I am a postdoctoral scholar at the Istituto Italiano di Tecnologia in Genova, Italy, working with Prof. Lorenzo Rosasco. I received my PhD in Mathematics from City University of Hong Kong, supervised by Prof. Han Feng and Prof. Ding-Xuan Zhou. I also visited Prof. Tomaso Poggio's group at MIT in September--October 2025.

My research lies at the interface of statistical learning theory, deep learning theory, approximation theory, and kernel methods. I am particularly interested in the mathematical analysis of deep neural networks, operator learning, functional data analysis, and learning problems with low-dimensional, sparse, or compositional structure.

Generalization analysis Operator learning Kernel methods Approximation theory

Research Interests

I study theoretical questions in modern machine learning, with an emphasis on how structure affects approximation, optimization, and generalization.

Theory of Deep Learning

Generalization analysis of deep neural networks, operator learning, and functional data analysis.

Approximation Theory

Spherical harmonics, neural network approximation, and structured high-dimensional function classes.

Statistical Learning Theory

Kernel methods, regression, classification, pairwise learning, and learning under structural assumptions.
